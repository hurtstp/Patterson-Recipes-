Pork Tenderloin with Garlic-Orange Vinaigrette and Sauteed Spinach

Pork, Seasonings, Sauces and Spices

Prep Time: 35 mins Cook Time: 35 mins Servings: Servings: 4 Source: Marthastewart.com

Ingredients

4 tablespoons olive oil

2 pork tenderloins (about 1 pound each), trimmed and patted dry

Coarse salt and ground pepper

1/4 cup orange juice

2 tablespoons Dijon mustard

1 tablespoon honey

1 garlic clove

4 bunches flat-leaf spinach (about 2 pounds total), thick stems removed, washed well

Directions

Preheat oven to 425 degrees. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon oil over medium; season pork with salt and pepper. Add pork to skillet, and cook, turning occasionally, until nicey browned on all sides. Transfer pork to a lined cookie sheet and roast until tested with thermometer and temp is about 125 degrees about 10 minutes. Remove from oven, cover and rest. (reserve skillet).

Meanwhile, using the imulsion blender, combine orange juice, mustard, honey, garlic, and 2 tablespoons oil. Season with salt and pepper, and blend until smooth; set vinaigrette aside.

In reserved skillet, heat remaining tablespoon oil over med/high. Add as much spinach as will fit; toss until wilted, adding more spinach as there is room, 3 to 5 minutes total. Drain off excess liquid, and season spinach with salt and pepper. Slice pork and drizzle with vinaigrette; serve with spinach.

Notes

For a fast, perfectly creamy vinaigrette, put ingredients in a blender, cover, and give them a whirl.
