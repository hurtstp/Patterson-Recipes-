New England Clam Chowder

★★★★★

Seafood, Soup & Chili & Stews

Servings: Yield: 6 to 8 servings Source: Foodnetwork.com

Ingredients

1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Bacon fat from 5 strips of bacon (~4 tbsp)

1 medium onion, finely diced

2 celery stalks, diced

1 tsp thyme, minced

4 tablespoons all-purpose flour

1 cup chicken broth

8 oz clam juice

3 (6.5 oz) cans chopped clams in juice

1-1/2 cups heavy cream

2 bay leaves

1 pound russet potatoes, cut into 1/2- inch cubes

Salt and freshly ground black pepper

5 strips bacon, chopped for topping

Directions

Heat the butter and bacon fat in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the onion, celery and thyme, and saute until softened, stirring often. Add flour, stirring to distribute evenly. Add the chicken stock, clam juice, juice from all cans of chopped clams (reserve clams), cream, bay leaves, and potatoes and stir to combine. Bring to a simmer while stirring (the mixture will thicken), then reduce the heat to low and cook 20 minutes, stirring often, until the potatoes are tender. Add clams and season to taste with salt and pepper. Cook until clams are just firm, about 2-4 minutes.

Serve with bacon bits sprinkled on top as garnish.
