Sour Cherry Strudels

Desserts

Prep Time: 30 minutes Cook Time: 1/2 hours Servings: 6 servings Source: Epicurious.com

Ingredients

5 cups fresh or frozen (not thawed) pitted sour cherries (1 1/2 lb)*

3/4 cup plus 1 1/2 teaspoons granulated sugar

2 1/2 tablespoons cornstarch

1 cinnamon stick

2 Sheets of puff pastry

1 stick (1/2 cup) unsalted butter, melted

Garnish: confectioners sugar

Accompaniment: vanilla ice cream or lightly sweetened whipped cream

Directions

Bring cherries (with any juices), 3/4 cup sugar, and cinnamon to a boil in a 2 1/2- to 3-quart heavy saucepan, stirring occasionally, then simmer 2 minutes. MIxe corn starch with 2 TBSP cold water. Add to cherry mixture. Stir until thickened and cherries are glossy. Transfer filling to a bowl and cool completely.

Preheat oven to 400°F.

Roll out pastry sheets to 12 x 14. Keep cold until ready to fill. With one short side of dough facing you, mound filling horizontally in a strip across the center, leaving a 1-inch border on both long sides. Fold top part, then bottom part of dough over filling. Turn pastry over, seam side down.
Transfer, seam side down, to a Parchment paper covered baking sheet. Brush top with some butter, then sprinkle lightly with 1/4 teaspoon sugar. Cut 2 (1-inch) vents diagonally across top of strudel, about 1 1/2 inches apart, with a paring knife. Make 5 more strudels in same manner.

Bake strudels in middle of oven until golden brown and crisp, 20 to 25 minutes. Transfer to a rack and cool 30 minutes.

We used IFQ (Individually Quick Frozen) cherries from Friske Orchards (231-599-2604), in Charlevoix, Michigan.

Notes

Strudels can be baked 2 hours ahead and kept on rack at room temperature.

Cherry filling can be made 1 day ahead and chilled, covered.
